  • Commission on air pollution in NCR to shut down as ordinance lapses.

    A commission to tackle air pollution in the National Capital Region will have to shut down only five months after it was formed because the ordinance that set it up has lapsed.“The commission will cease to function but it is not as if the issue of air pollution will go unaddressed,” environment secretary RP Gupta told ET. “Institutions such as the Central Pollution Control Board, the state pollution control boards and state governments of Delhi, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan, and the ministry of environment will continue to deal with issues relating to monitoring and management of air quality.”The move came within days of the government indicating to the Supreme Court that it was considering an institutional measure to address air pollution in the NCR.
